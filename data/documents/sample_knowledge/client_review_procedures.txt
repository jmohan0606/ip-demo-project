Client Review and Suitability Procedures (Policy CR-7, version 3.1)

Scope: applies to all client-facing advisors and their supervising principals for
annual reviews, material account changes, and any recommendation of a managed or
advisory program.

1. Annual review requirement.
Every advised household must receive a documented review at least once every 12 months.
The review record must capture: current objectives, risk tolerance confirmation or
update, material life changes (employment, health, family, liquidity needs), and any
recommendation made. Reviews conducted but not documented are treated as not conducted.

2. Suitability documentation.
No managed account, advisory program, or discretionary mandate may be recommended
without a current suitability assessment on file, dated within the preceding 12 months.
The assessment must evidence: investment objective, time horizon, liquidity needs, risk
profile, and the basis for concluding the recommended program fits them. A risk profile
of conservative is incompatible with concentrated or leveraged strategies absent a
documented, client-acknowledged exception approved by a principal.

3. Supervisory review thresholds.
Recommendations with estimated revenue impact or transfer value at or above $50,000
require supervisory principal review before presentation to the client. The reviewing
principal must be independent of the recommending advisor's production credit.

4. Prohibited representations.
Advisors must not state or imply guaranteed returns, assured outperformance, or
risk-free outcomes in any client communication, in any channel. Comparative claims
against named competitors require compliance pre-approval.

5. Fee transparency.
At the point of recommendation, the client must receive the program fee schedule and a
plain-language statement of how the advisor is compensated. For transfers from
brokerage to advisory, the cost comparison worksheet is mandatory.

6. Record retention.
Review records, suitability assessments, and recommendation rationales are retained for
seven years and must be producible within 10 business days of a regulatory or internal
audit request.

Escalation: suspected departures from this policy go to the district compliance officer
within one business day of discovery. Good-faith self-reports are treated as mitigating.
