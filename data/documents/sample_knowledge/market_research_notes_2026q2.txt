Market Research Notes — 2026 Q2 Wealth Management Landscape

Prepared by the practice intelligence team for field leadership. Internal use;
directional observations, not client-facing claims.

Client behavior shifts observed this quarter:
- Cash remains sticky. Even with money-market yields off their 2024 peak, households
  are holding 12-18% of investable assets in cash equivalents, versus a historical
  norm near 8%. Advisors report the effective conversation is reframing cash as a
  bucketed allocation (near-term reserve vs. long-term drag) rather than pitching
  products against it.
- Fee sensitivity is concentrated, not general. Households under $500k challenge
  advisory fees more often than a year ago; multi-million-dollar households continue
  to prioritize planning depth, tax coordination, and responsiveness over headline
  fee levels.
- Intergenerational transfer is no longer theoretical. Inheriting spouses and adult
  children now appear in a meaningful share of review meetings. Books where the
  advisor knows only the primary account holder are measurably more fragile.

Competitive landscape:
- Digital-first entrants keep winning the small-balance segment on onboarding speed,
  but their retention weakens when clients hit planning complexity (equity comp,
  business sale, estate questions). That hand-off moment is the traditional
  advisor's clearest win-back window.
- Regional bank programs are recruiting aggressively on payout, producing churn in
  mid-market territories. Client retention through advisor transitions has been
  strongest where a second team member already had an established relationship.

Implications for practice management:
1. Managed-account penetration remains the durable revenue story; books above 35%
   managed share show materially lower revenue volatility quarter to quarter.
2. The reviews-to-referrals loop is the cheapest growth channel in this environment;
   see the prospecting playbook for execution detail.
3. Team-based coverage of top households is now a retention control, not a luxury.

Watch items for Q3: money-market reallocation behavior if short rates fall further,
and the pace of advisory-fee compression in the under-$500k segment.
