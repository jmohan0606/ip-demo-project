CRM Engagement Guide: Pipeline Hygiene and Follow-Up Discipline

Purpose: Give advisors and their district leaders a common standard for working leads,
referrals, and opportunities inside the CRM so that pipeline value reflects reality and
no committed follow-up is silently dropped.

Lead handling standards:
- Every new lead must be acknowledged within 2 business days of assignment. Leads
  untouched for 10 business days are flagged to the district director.
- Record the lead source (client referral, partner referral, marketing campaign,
  walk-in, event) at creation. Referral-sourced leads convert at roughly twice the
  rate of campaign leads and should be prioritized in the weekly call block.
- A lead moves to qualified only after a documented discovery conversation covering
  household assets, current provider relationships, and a stated financial need.

Follow-up discipline:
- Any activity marked "follow-up required" must carry a due date. An overdue follow-up
  is a coaching signal, not just a task: three or more overdue follow-ups on one book
  correlates with stalled pipeline and should trigger a pipeline review conversation.
- After every client or prospect meeting, log a summary activity within 24 hours,
  including next step, owner, and date. Meetings without a logged next step are the
  single largest cause of pipeline leakage observed in field reviews.

Opportunity stage standards:
- An opportunity's amount must be an evidence-based estimate (stated investable assets
  or documented account transfer intent), never a placeholder. Review amounts at every
  stage change.
- Weighted pipeline value = amount x stage probability. Stage probabilities are set
  firm-wide (Discovery 20%, Proposal 45%, Verbal Commit 75%, Closing 90%) and must not
  be overridden per opportunity.
- Opportunities inactive for 30 days should be moved to stalled and either re-engaged
  with a dated plan or closed-lost honestly. A clean pipeline a leader can trust beats
  an inflated one every time.

Weekly cadence for advisors:
1. Monday: clear or reschedule every overdue follow-up before booking new activity.
2. Midweek: work the qualified-lead call block, referrals first.
3. Friday: update opportunity stages and amounts; verify the weighted pipeline number
   you would stand behind in a district review.
