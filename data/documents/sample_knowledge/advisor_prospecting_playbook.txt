Advisor Prospecting Playbook: Building Referral-Led Growth

Who this is for: advisors whose new-household acquisition has slowed, or whose net new
money depends on a small number of existing relationships.

Core principle: in wealth management, the warmest path to a new household is an
introduction from a satisfied client or a center of influence (CPA, estate attorney,
business banker). Cold outreach has its place, but referral-led prospecting compounds.

Play 1 — The post-review referral ask:
Immediately after a well-received annual review, when value delivered is concrete and
fresh, ask a specific question: "Who else in your family or circle is facing a decision
like the one we just worked through?" Specific beats generic; "anyone you know" invites
a polite no. Log the resulting names as referral-sourced leads the same day.

Play 2 — Center-of-influence mapping:
List the CPAs and attorneys already attached to your top 20 households. Offer them a
quarterly touchpoint on shared clients (with client consent). One reciprocal referral
relationship with an active CPA typically outproduces a season of seminar marketing.

Play 3 — Niche depth over breadth:
Advisors with a recognizable niche (medical practices, corporate executives with equity
compensation, small-business owners approaching a sale) win referrals because clients
can describe them in one sentence. Pick the niche your current book already leans
toward and build your next 90 days of outreach around it.

Play 4 — The dormant-lead resurrection sweep:
Quarterly, pull every lead marked lost or gone-quiet in the past 18 months. Situations
change: liquidity events, inheritances, dissatisfaction with the incumbent. A short
"thinking of you, here is one relevant idea" note re-opens more doors than advisors
expect, at nearly zero cost.

Measuring what matters:
- New qualified households per quarter (target: 3-5 for a maturing book).
- Referral ratio: share of new leads that are referral-sourced (healthy books run 50%+).
- Conversion time from qualified lead to funded account; watch for stalls at proposal.

Discipline note: prospecting activity belongs in the CRM like any other pipeline work —
sourced, staged, and followed up on schedule. See the CRM Engagement Guide for the
follow-up and staging standards that apply to prospect records.
