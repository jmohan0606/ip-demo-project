Advisor Growth Plan (AGP) Program Overview — Field Edition

What the AGP is: a 12-month, milestone-based growth program agreed between each advisor
and their market director at the start of the program year. It converts the advisor's
annual revenue and asset goals into quarterly milestones and monthly KPI expectations,
so that "on track" or "off track" is a matter of evidence, not opinion.

Program structure:
- Goal layer: full-year targets for production revenue, net new money (NNM), and
  managed-asset share, set relative to the advisor's trailing year and market averages.
- Milestone layer: quarterly checkpoints (Month 3, 6, 9, 12), each with an attainment
  percentage. Milestone attainment below 85% at any checkpoint moves the advisor to
  the attention tier; below 70% is urgent and requires a written recovery plan.
- KPI layer: the monthly execution indicators that predict milestone attainment —
  client review completion rate, CRM follow-up currency (no overdue follow-ups older
  than 10 days), qualified-lead additions, and managed-account proposal count.

On-track / off-track scoring:
The AGP risk score combines milestone attainment, KPI on-track ratio, and activity
recency into a 0-100 scale. Scores under 40 are considered on track; 40-60 warrants
attention; above 60 is off track and triggers a coaching conversation with the market
director within two weeks. The score exists to focus coaching where the plan is
actually slipping — it is not a compensation input.

Coaching cadence:
- Monthly: advisor and district director review the KPI layer (15 minutes, evidence
  from the dashboard, not recollection).
- Quarterly: milestone review; celebrate what is genuinely ahead of plan, agree the
  one or two execution changes most likely to close any gap.
- Recovery plans name specific activities and dates (e.g., "complete the 12 overdue
  reviews by March 15"), never generic intentions ("improve activity").

What good looks like by year end:
Milestone attainment at or above 95%, KPI on-track ratio above 0.75, NNM positive in
at least three of four quarters, and a managed-asset share moving toward the advisor's
agreed mix target. Advisors who hit these markers two years running are the program's
mentor pool for the incoming class.
